TIME: 0.00 to 3.00 Welcome to Finding Your Groove Contributing to Cpython. I'm Carol Willing and I'm not a CPython core committer nor do I moonlight as one at the Montreal Python User Group meetings. Yet, I am a genuine contributor to CPython.

Commit

Perhaps you daydream about the celebration as your "CPython Golden Commit" is merged. You can hear the trumpets and the jazz vocalist singing the source code of your succesful "golden commit". Oh wait, that's not the type of commit that this talk is about. While a "Golden Commit" may be the end result of your personal commitment, not all CPython journeys have a goal of a commit. In fact, my personal journey does not.

Jump into it

My personal journey and commitment to the Python community is to do my best to onboard developers and help them reach their goals through outreach and education. This talk pulls together wisdom from many different Python contributors and users (core developers, contributors, writers, testers, developers, users, outreach instructors).

Find your rhythm

I will share tips with you on how you may contribute to CPython, what it means, where to look for something to work on, how to be more effective as a contributor, and demystify the process enough that you will consider jumping into the upcoming sprints.

Improvise

I am not going to share hidden details about source code on a particular library, but I will share with you resources that will inspire, encourage and help you be resourceful and improvise your Python path.
